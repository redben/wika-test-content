Garden Scramble

03-01-2010

How many times have i found myself the owner of a hodge-podge of vegetables....a little bit of this and a little bit of that? usually i throw the bits and pieces into a soup, or end up making vegetable stock. but this time, i had guests, and had to feed them breakfast....so i ended up with this, served with canadian bacon and toast! feel free to change out the veggies, using what you have! **if you find yourself the proud owner of some leftover roasted veggies...just heat them up and use those and skip the first step...they work wonderfully!

  • Saute the first 5 ingredients in 2 tbsp butter in a killet over medium heat , stirring constantly until tender
  • Set aside
  • Keep warm
  • Combine eggs , milk , basil , and savory
  • Beat with a wire whisk until blended
  • Melt 1 tbsp butter in a large skillet over low heat
  • Add egg mixture , and cook over medium-low heat , without stirring , until mixture begins to set on the bottom
  • Draw a spatula across the bottom of the pan to form large curds
  • Continue cooking until eggs are firm but still moist -- do not stir constantly ! gently stir in reserved sauteed vegetables into egg mixture
  • Sprinkle with cheese
  • Serve immediately
